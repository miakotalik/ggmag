Girl Genius is a 501(c)(3) aiming to create an inclusive community where people of color in STEAM (non-binary and trans-inclusive) are given a voice and empower the next generation of female leaders.
OUR PROJECTS

How We Inspire Girls In STEAM Year Round.

We are expanding our initiative beyond our magazine to provide free opportunities to our growing community of girls interested in STEM.

Magazine

Our team works together to create empowering issues for our audience with articles on women in STEAM, breaking into tech, and tackling imposter syndrome.

Blog

Our blog posts on Medium provide insight securing internships, organizing hackathons, and being represented in STEM fields.

YouTube Channel

Our YouTube channel features introspective interviews with female change makers in STEAM and give advice on how to navigate high school and college.

Virtual Events

We are collaborating with nationally acclaimed nonprofits to hold free panels and workshops, like How To Build A Business.

OUR MEDIUMS OF IMPACT

Our Passion Projects

We strive to provide our community with resources so they can discover their inner leader and create a positive impact in their schools and communities.

Take a Class, Save a Life

100% of profits are donated to healthcare workers and those affected by the Coronavirus.

How it works

For a limited time, we are offering donation-based classes.

  1. Pick a class you like
  2. Donate what you think is fair
  3. Learn how to code!

Students in Elementary and Middle School are eligible to sign up for our live, instructor-led courses. The class is taught by our talented volunteers. We then donate all proceeds to charitable organizations providing direct relief for the pandemic. In the interest of transparency, we will continue to keep an updated list of charities we are contributing to on our website.

Volunteer Run Organization

Quarantine Coders is proudly run by a group of independent volunteers who are passionate about social change. Our instructors are all volunteers who either work full time in the tech industry or are Computer Science students at some of the top colleges in the nation.
